# Style Routing

Use this matrix after inspecting the photos. The route should feel derived from the trip, not pasted on top.

## Natural History Museum

- Tone: clean, light science, soft parent-child curiosity.
- Palette: mist blue, pale cyan, mint, cream white; small amber highlights for fossils and warm exhibit lights.
- Motifs: engraved dinosaurs, fossils, shells, minerals, leaves, planets, magnifier marks.
- Photos: dinosaur skeletons and halls as anchors; child looking up or pointing; mineral, insect, fossil, leaf, texture details.
- Copy: short playful science lines, e.g. `地球小探险家`, `解锁远古世界`, `今天认识一块石头`.
- Avoid: dark retro filters, heavy historical fonts, crowded cute stickers.

## Calligraphy / Painting Gallery

- Tone: quiet, airy, literati, soft ink.
- Palette: warm rice paper, muted ink green, pale sage, light ochre, soft brown.
- Motifs: brush lines, seals, unfolded scrolls, window lattice, ink leaves, inscription slips.
- Photos: gallery entrance, full wall works, child/adult viewing from side/back, brush or seal details.
- Copy: restrained poetic lines, e.g. `先慢下来`, `看一幅大字`, `一纸山河`.
- Avoid: saturated colors, cartoon stickers, loud film grain.

## History / Cultural Museum

- Tone: calm documentary, aged but not gloomy.
- Palette: kraft, caramel, clay, dark vermilion, muted bronze, warm gray.
- Motifs: bronze patterns, pottery silhouettes, seals, coins, archive labels, carved border fragments.
- Photos: artifact cases, reconstructed streets, reliefs, child/adult pausing, bronze/ceramic/detail close-ups.
- Copy: short historical feeling, e.g. `踏寻千年`, `触摸时间`, `一城一史`.
- Avoid: macaron colors, childish doodles, overly bright filters.

## Zoo / Animal Trip

- Tone: lively field notes, warm and playful, not childish clutter.
- Palette: leaf green, oat, sky blue, terracotta, warm cream.
- Motifs: field-guide line drawings of animals seen in photos, paw marks, leaves, binoculars, map pins.
- Photos: full-body animal views, interaction moments, child/adult watching from behind, habitat details.
- Copy: observation diary lines, e.g. `今天遇见长颈鹿`, `慢慢看它走路`, `给大象留一分钟`.
- Avoid: generic zoo clipart unrelated to actual animals, neon colors, dense sticker piles.

## Art / Contemporary Exhibition

- Tone: clean gallery, editorial, slightly graphic.
- Palette: white, charcoal, muted accent sampled from artwork; one accent color only.
- Motifs: gallery labels, thin arrows, frame marks, geometric traces from exhibits.
- Photos: installation-wide view, viewer silhouette, artwork detail, texture/lighting.
- Copy: concise viewing notes, e.g. `先看形状`, `颜色停了一秒`, `走进一束光`.
- Avoid: over-decorating the art, cartoon stickers that compete with the exhibit.

## City / Travel Day

- Tone: route journal, postcard, light travel notes.
- Palette: match destination: coast uses mint/sky/sand; old town uses stone/green/brick; city night uses charcoal/cream/accent.
- Motifs: maps, tickets, transit lines, simple landmarks, weather marks.
- Photos: route scenes, food stops, architecture, people walking, small found details.
- Copy: time-route snippets, e.g. `拐进一条小路`, `今天的风很轻`.
- Avoid: one-size-fits-all travel icons detached from the city.

## Lifestyle / Adult Travel

- Tone: more editorial and calm; less cartoon, more negative space.
- Palette: derived from outfits, architecture, and food tones.
- Motifs: tickets, handwritten labels, fine line florals, object sketches from the photos.
- Photos: strongest atmosphere first; detail shots balance people shots.
- Copy: restrained sensory notes, e.g. `把下午留给窗边`, `走到这里刚好`.
- Avoid: childlike type unless the user asks for it.
